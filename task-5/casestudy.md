# Phishing Case Study

Phishing is a type of cybercrime where attackers trick users into sharing sensitive information such as passwords, bank details, or OTPs by pretending to be a trusted source.

A common scenario involves receiving a fake email or message that appears to be from a bank or UPI service. The message contains a link that redirects to a fake website. When the user enters their details, attackers gain access and may misuse the information.

Phishing mainly targets students and online users. The consequences include financial loss, identity theft, and misuse of personal data.

This highlights the importance of verifying links and avoiding suspicious messages.
