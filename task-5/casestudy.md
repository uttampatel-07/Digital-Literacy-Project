Phishing is a type of cybercrime in which attackers trick individuals
into revealing sensitive information such as passwords, bank details, or
OTPs by pretending to be a trusted source. These attacks are commonly
carried out through fake emails, SMS messages, or websites that closely
resemble legitimate ones.

A typical phishing scenario involves a student receiving a message that
appears to be from a bank, UPI service, or popular website. The message
urges the user to verify account details or claim a reward by clicking
on a link. This link redirects to a fake website that looks identical to
the original. When the user enters login credentials or OTP, the
attacker gains access to their account.

Phishing attacks mainly target students and young users because they
frequently use online platforms and may not always verify the
authenticity of messages. These attacks can lead to serious consequences
such as financial loss, identity theft, data misuse, and loss of
personal privacy.

In many real-life cases, users have lost money due to fake payment
requests or scam links shared via messaging apps. Attackers often create
urgency to pressure users into taking quick action without thinking.

To prevent such attacks, users should always check the sender's details,
verify URLs, avoid clicking suspicious links, and never share OTPs or
passwords. Awareness and careful behavior are the most effective ways to
stay safe from phishing.

This case study highlights the importance of digital awareness and
responsible online behavior in preventing cybercrime.
